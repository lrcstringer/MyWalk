# MyWalk — Complete User Guide

---

## What is MyWalk?

MyWalk is a Christian discipleship app designed to help you grow spiritually through consistent daily habits, biblical study, reflective journaling, and community accountability. It is built around the conviction that spiritual growth is not passive — it requires intentional, regular practice sustained over time.

The app brings together five interconnected disciplines:

- **Daily habit tracking** — building and maintaining spiritual practices
- **Progress analytics** — seeing your consistency and celebrating milestones
- **Private journaling** — reflecting deeply on your spiritual journey
- **Biblical content** — studying the Fruit of the Spirit, the Beatitudes, the Parables of Jesus, the "I Am" sayings, and more
- **Prayer Circles** — growing alongside a trusted community

MyWalk is organized around a five-tab bottom navigation bar: **Today**, **Progress**, **Journal**, **Kingdom Life**, and **Circles**.

---

## Getting Started: Onboarding

When you open MyWalk for the first time, you will be guided through a short setup:

1. **Welcome** — Introduction to the app and its purpose.
2. **Sign In** — Sign in with your Apple ID or Google account. This is required for Circles and to sync your data across devices.
3. **Your Identity** — Enter your display name and an optional bio.
4. **Reframe** — A brief reflection on who you are in Christ and what you are hoping to grow into.
5. **First Gratitude** — Record your first gratitude entry, establishing the practice from day one.
6. **Choose Your Habits** — Browse a library of pre-built habit templates and select the ones you want to start with. Free users may select up to 2 habits.
7. **Set Up Your Habits** — Configure each selected habit: set your daily target, active days, tracking type, and purpose statement.
8. **Week Dedication** — Commit your first week to God — a brief moment of prayer and intention-setting that will repeat each Sunday.

After onboarding, you will be prompted to allow notifications so MyWalk can remind you to check in on your habits.

## The Week Rhythm

MyWalk is structured around a weekly spiritual rhythm:

- **Sunday — Week Dedication:** Each Sunday, you are invited to dedicate the coming week to God. This is a brief ceremonial commitment that sets the tone for your week. If you miss Sunday, the dedication carries forward automatically (shown as a banner on the Today tab).
- **Start of a New Week — Week Look-Back:** When you open the app at the start of a new week (any day after the previous week has ended), MyWalk shows a full-screen "Your Week in Review" before you reach the main tabs. It covers your check-in score, a per-habit breakdown, any milestones you hit, a Fruit of the Spirit summary, and a grace message. Tapping "Dedicate this week" dismisses it and moves you into the new week's dedication.
- **Daily Check-Ins:** Each day you open the app, your habits are waiting to be completed on the Today tab.

## Tab 1: Today

The **Today** tab is the heart of your daily practice. This is where you track your habits each day and see how you are doing.

### The Habit Card

Each habit you have created appears as a card on the Today tab. A card shows:

- The habit name and category icon
- Your purpose statement (a short personal "why")
- The tracking type and daily target
- The days of the week the habit is active
- A completion button or input field

**Completing a habit:**
- **Check-in habits:** Tap the checkmark button to mark the habit complete for today.
- **Timed habits:** Enter the number of minutes you spent (e.g., 30 minutes of Bible reading). You can log partial values and add more later in the day.
- **Count habits:** Enter the number of times you completed the action (e.g., ×3 gratitude entries).
- **Abstain habits:** Tap to confirm you avoided the behavior for today.

You can undo your last entry by tapping the undo option that briefly appears after logging.

### Navigating Days

A **week strip** at the top of the Today tab shows the current week. Tap any day to view your check-ins for that day — useful for reviewing past entries or catching up on a missed day.

### Adding a New Habit

Tap the **+** button (or "Add Habit" button) at the bottom of the Today tab to open the habit creation flow.

**Basic settings:**
- **Name** — What you want to call your habit.
- **Category** — Choose from: Scripture & Prayer, Exercise & Movement, Rest & Sleep, Fasting, Study & Learning, Service & Generosity, Connection, Health & Nourishment, Breaking Bad Habits, Gratitude, or Custom.
- **Tracking type** — Check-in, Timed, Count, or Abstain.
- **Purpose statement** — A one-sentence "why" that will appear on your habit card every day. Pre-filled options are available per category, or write your own.

**Advanced settings (Premium):**
- **Daily target** — For timed habits (minutes) or count habits (number of reps, miles, etc.).
- **Target unit** — Label for your count (e.g., "minutes", "pages", "reps").
- **Active days** — Which days of the week the habit is active. Deselect days you do not want to be prompted.
- **Trigger** — The cue that leads to this habit (e.g., "After morning coffee").
- **Coping plan** — What you will do when you feel like skipping.
- **Notes** — Private notes attached to this habit (encrypted).
- **Reference URL** — A link to a resource (article, podcast, etc.) related to the habit.
- **Fruit of the Spirit** — Tag this habit with one or more of the nine fruits. This connects your habit to your Fruit of the Spirit portfolio.
- **Prayer items** — Toggle to add structured prayer prompts to this habit.
- **Accountability partner** — Invite someone to follow your progress on this specific habit.

Free users may create up to **2 habits**. Premium users have no limit.

### Habit Detail View

Tap a habit card to open the full detail view. Here you can:

- See all habit settings and your purpose statement
- View a heatmap of your past completions
- See total stats (days completed, total value logged)
- Log a check-in directly
- Edit or delete the habit
- Add a journal entry linked to this habit
- View or manage your accountability partner for this habit

### Editing or Deleting a Habit

From the Habit Detail View, tap the edit icon to modify any setting. To delete a habit, use the delete option in the edit view. You can also archive a habit to remove it from Today without permanently deleting the history.

### Gratitude Share

If you are part of a Prayer Circle, a brief prompt may appear to share your gratitude with your circle after completing a gratitude-related habit.

### Settings

Tap the **gear icon** in the top-right of the Today tab to access app settings, including notification preferences, your profile, and account options (sign out, delete account).

## Tab 2: Progress

The **Progress** tab shows you how you are doing over time — both this week and across your entire journey.

### This Week

- **Week Score** — Your overall daily completion rate for the current week, shown as a percentage. This is the average of your daily scores across all active habits.
- **Performance Tier** — A label based on your score (e.g., Faithful, Steady, Growing).
- **Completions** — Total habit check-ins this week out of the total possible.
- **Upcoming Milestones** — A preview of milestones you are close to reaching (e.g., "2 more days until your 7-day streak on Morning Prayer").

### Journey Stats

Cumulative numbers across your entire time using the app:

- **Giving Days** — Total number of times you completed a service or generosity habit.
- **Total Check-ins** — All habit completions ever logged.
- **Gratitude Days** — Total days you completed a gratitude habit.
- **Milestones Reached** — Number of milestone celebrations you have earned.
- **Prayers Answered** — Prayers you have marked as answered in your Circles.

### Heatmap

The heatmap shows a grid of your habit completions over time. Each cell represents a day; the darker the color, the higher your completion rate that day.

- **Free users** see 1 week of history.
- **Premium users** see up to 52 weeks (one full year) of history.

Scroll left or right to move through weeks. The heatmap gives you a visual picture of your consistency over time.

## Tab 3: Journal

The **Journal** tab is a private, encrypted space for reflection. Everything you write here is visible only to you.

### Writing a Journal Entry

Tap the **compose button** (pencil icon) to create a new entry. The composer supports:

- **Text** — A rich multi-line text editor. Write freely.
- **Voice recording** — Tap the microphone icon to record a voice note. The app will transcribe your recording to text automatically. Playback is available in the entry.
- **Photos** — Tap the camera or gallery icon to attach a photo from your device.
- **Drawing** — Tap the draw icon to open a freehand canvas. Sketch, annotate, or doodle directly in your entry.

### Linking to a Habit or Fruit

When creating an entry, you can link it to a specific habit (so it appears in that habit's detail view) or tag it with a Fruit of the Spirit (so it appears in that fruit's detail view). You can also create a standalone free entry with no links.

### Browsing Entries

The journal list shows all your entries in reverse chronological order. You can:

- **Pin** an entry to keep it at the top of the list.
- **Search** entries by text or linked habit name.
- **Filter** by Fruit of the Spirit tag.
- **Sort** by date or pinned status.

### Entry Detail

Tap any entry to read it in full. From the detail view you can edit, delete, or share the entry. Attached photos and voice recordings are playable directly in the detail view.

### Privacy & Encryption

All journal text is encrypted end-to-end — MyWalk cannot read your entries. The first time you open the Journal tab, a brief explanation of this privacy model is shown. Your journal is yours alone.

## Tab 4: Kingdom Life

### Fruit of the Spirit *(Free)*

**Galatians 5:22–23** — "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control."

Tap **Fruit of the Spirit** to open your Fruit Portfolio — a visual display of all nine fruits.

**Each fruit card shows:**
- The Greek word (e.g., *agapē* for Love)
- A symbolic icon and color
- A daily check-in prompt (a question to reflect on)
- A completion message
- A key anchor verse

**Tapping a fruit** opens its full detail view, which includes:
- **Statement in Brief** — A concise theological summary of what this fruit means.
- **Central Point** — The core insight Paul is communicating.
- **The Question It Asks You** — A personal reflection prompt.
- **Suggested Practices** — Three concrete weekly practices to embody this fruit.
- **Audience Context** — Why Paul wrote this to the Galatians specifically.
- **Historical Context** — Background on the first-century setting.
- **Scholarly Interpretation** — How theologians understand this fruit.
- **Exegetical Notes** — Notes on the original Greek text.

**Add a [Fruit] Practice button** — Opens the Micro-Actions Library, a curated list of practical habits you can add to your Today tab for this fruit. Each micro-action includes a name, description, purpose statement, tracking type (daily/weekly, check-in/timed/count), and an anchor verse. Tap any action to see full details and add it as a habit.

**The nine fruits and their micro-actions:**

| Fruit | Practices Available |
|-------|-------------------|
| Love | Write a note of appreciation · Pray for someone difficult · Give your full attention · Serve without being asked · Reach out to someone lonely |
| Joy | Record moments of delight · Rest in God's presence · Laugh with someone · Memorise a joy-filled verse · Praise God physically |
| Peace | Take one step toward peace · Digital rest before bed · Sit in silence · Release a worry in prayer |
| Patience | Pause before responding · Wait without your phone · Pray for the one who frustrated you · Read slowly and deeply · Journal without an agenda |
| Kindness | Do one kind thing for a stranger · Speak one encouraging word · Bring a meal or gift · Notice the overlooked · Volunteer an hour |
| Goodness | Keep a private commitment · Address one small dishonesty · Give without recognition · Take one hard right step · Spend time with the overlooked |
| Faithfulness | Show up on a hard day · Keep a small promise · Read the Bible every day · Give faithfully · Be reliable for someone |
| Gentleness | Lower your voice · Speak gently to yourself · Respond, not react · Listen without fixing · Pray with open hands |
| Self-Control | Delay a craving · Limit social media · Sleep at a consistent time · Fast from what controls you · Guard your words |

You can also create your own custom practice from within any fruit tab.

## Tab 5: Circles

The **Circles** tab is the community hub of MyWalk. Prayer Circles are small groups of trusted people — friends, family, a small group, a discipleship cohort — who walk the spiritual journey together.

**Sign in required:** Circles requires you to be signed in with an Apple or Google account. If you are not signed in, the Circles tab will show you an overview of the features and a sign-in button.

---

### Creating a Circle

Tap **Create Circle** to start a new community.

- Enter a **name** and **description** for your circle.
- Configure **permissions:**
  - Who can edit the scripture focus (admin only, or any member)
  - Who can create events (admin only, or any member)
  - Whether the weekly pulse shows named or anonymous responses
  - Whether encouragement prompts are enabled
  - Whether the habit grid shows checkmarks only or member names

Once created, your circle has a unique **invite code**. Share this code with people you want to invite. They enter the code under **Join Circle**.

---

### Joining a Circle

Tap **Join Circle** and enter the invite code you received. You will be added to the circle immediately.

---

### Circle Detail View

Tap any circle in your list to open it. The circle detail view has multiple tabs:

---

#### Activity Feed

A stream of recent activity from everyone in the circle: habit milestones achieved, encouragements sent, prayer requests added, announcements from leaders, and gratitude posts. This is the social heartbeat of your circle.

---

#### Prayer List

The Prayer List has two sections:

**Group Prayer Items** — Circle-wide prayer needs posted by the leader or members (depending on permissions):
- Add a prayer item with a title, optional memo, and visibility settings
- Track status: Praying / Answered / Unanswered
- Reorder items by priority

**Prayer Requests** — Personal requests from individual members:
- Post a request (text, optional duration: this week / ongoing / until removed)
- Other members see how many people have prayed for each request
- Mark a request as answered and add an answer note
- The prayer count badge shows total members who have prayed

**To pray for a request:** Tap the prayer icon on any request card. This increments the prayer count and records your prayer.

**To add a request:** Tap the **+ Add Request** button and enter the text and duration.

---

#### Scripture Threads

A space for shared scripture reflection within the circle.

- The leader (or any member, depending on permissions) posts a scripture reference with a passage and commentary.
- Members can reply in threaded comments.
- Threads can be open (anyone can comment) or closed (read-only).
- Comments support rich text formatting.

**To add a scripture thread:** Tap the **+ Add** button, enter the reference, paste or type the passage, and add your commentary.

**To comment:** Tap any thread to open it, then type in the comment field at the bottom.

---

#### Circle Habits

Shared habits for the whole circle to practice together.

- The circle can have habits that all members track — visible to everyone.
- Each habit shows a daily completion grid with all members' check-ins.
- Completion rates and participation percentages are visible.
- Habits can be daily, weekly, or set to specific days.

**To add a circle habit:** Tap **+ Add Habit** (admin permission required by default) and configure the habit name, tracking type, frequency, target, and anchor verse.

**To check in:** Your personal completion row appears in the grid. Tap to log your check-in for today.

---

#### Events

Upcoming events for the circle.

- Event cards show title, date, time, location (or meeting link), and description.
- Tap **Add to Calendar** to add the event to your device's calendar.
- A reminder badge shows if you have set a reminder.

**To create an event:** Tap **+ Add Event** and fill in the details.

---

#### Additional Circle Features

**Gratitude Wall**
A weekly feed of gratitude posts from circle members. You can post anonymously or with your name. Gratitude entries flow from your Today tab — when you complete a gratitude habit, you are offered the option to share it with the circle.

**Sunday Summary (Weekly Pulse)**
A weekly wellness check-in for the whole circle. Each week, members answer: *How are you doing spiritually this week?*

Options:
- Encouraged
- Steady
- Struggling
- Needs Prayer

Members can optionally add a brief note. Responses can be anonymous or named, depending on circle settings. The summary shows aggregate totals so the group can see how everyone is doing and identify anyone who needs extra support.

**To complete your pulse:** Tap the Sunday Summary card and select your status.

**Encouragements**
Send a quick word of encouragement to any circle member. Tap the encouragement button on a member's activity, then choose from eight pre-set templates or write your own custom message. You can send anonymously.

Pre-set templates include:
- "Praying for you today"
- "Keep going — you're doing great"
- "God sees your faithfulness"
- "Proud of you"
- "You're not walking alone"
- "Praying God gives you strength"
- "Thinking of you"
- "Grateful to be in community"

**Milestone Celebrations**
When a circle member reaches a habit milestone (a streak, a total count, a time goal), it appears in the Activity Feed as a celebration card. Other members can react to celebrate together.

**Circle Heatmap**
A visual grid showing the entire circle's combined habit completion history. Free circles see 1 week; premium circles see up to 52 weeks.

**Collective Milestones**
Running totals for the circle as a whole:
- Total service/giving days across all members
- Total hours logged across all timed habits
- Total gratitude days
- Other collective achievements

## Accountability Partnerships

For any individual habit, you can invite one person to be your accountability partner for that habit.

**To invite a partner:**
1. Open the Habit Detail View.
2. Tap **Invite Accountability Partner**.
3. A unique invite link is generated: `mywalk.faith/accountability/accept/[token]`
4. Share this link with your chosen partner via any messaging app.

**Your partner will:**
- Receive the link and be shown the habit details.
- Accept or decline the partnership.
- If they accept, they can see your daily check-in status for that habit.

**Once active, a partnership includes:**
- Your partner's completion status visible to you.
- A private message thread between you and your partner for that habit.
- Option to end the partnership at any time from the Habit Detail View.

**Partnership statuses:** Pending → Active → Ended (or Declined)

## Privacy & Security

- **Journal entries** are encrypted end-to-end. MyWalk cannot read your journal.
- **Habit notes** are also encrypted.
- **Circle data** is stored in Firebase and visible only to circle members.
- **Sign in** is handled by Apple or Google — MyWalk never stores your password.
- **Delete Account** — Available in Settings. This permanently removes all your data from MyWalk's servers.
- **Reset All Data** — Available in Settings. Clears all local data without deleting your account.

---

## Notifications

MyWalk can send you reminders for:
- Individual habits (configurable per habit)
- Circle activity (prayer requests, encouragements, milestones)
- Weekly pulse reminders
- Milestone celebrations

To manage notifications, go to **Today → Settings (gear icon) → Notifications**.

---

## Offline Use

MyWalk is designed to work offline. Your habits and journal are available without a network connection. Any entries or check-ins made offline are synced automatically when your connection returns. The full Bible database is stored locally on your device and does not require internet access.

Journal media (photos and voice recordings) are queued for upload when offline and uploaded automatically when the connection is restored.
